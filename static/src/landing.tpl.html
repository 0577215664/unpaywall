Get open-access versions of paywalled research papers, instantly.
Unpaywall is a Chrome browser extension that automatically links you to free fulltext when you hit paywalls, using our index of over ten million legal, open-access articles.
Install it free on the Chrome Web Store

FAQ

Who's behind Unpaywall?
We're Impactstory, a nonprofit working to supercharge science by making it more open, reusable, and web-native.
Why did you make it?
We got tired of running into paywalled articles! The worst part was, we knew that millions of researchers are currently uploading their own fulltext PDFs to preprint servers and institutional repositories, worldwide, making them free for anyone to read. There was just no easy way to find them, especially not one that was integrated into our reading workflows. So we made one! Over the longer term, our goal for Unpaywall is to nurture the transition to a fully open access scholarly publishing model, by closing the gap between readers and freely-available fulltext.
How do I use this?
After you install the extension we'll do the work in the background. When you view a paywalled article, we'll search for a free legal copy. When we find one (about 25% of the time right now), you'll see an "unlock" tab on the right of your browser. Click the tab, read the article. Easy.
What do the different tab colors mean?
When we find a free PDF, we use the color of the tab to show where we got it from:
  • Gold articles are available from the publisher under an open license. (example)
  • Blue articles are available on the current page, either because they're open-access without a license, or you're browsing from behind the paywall. (example)
  • Green articles are available via an open, legal preprint server or institutional repository. (example)
How often does Unpaywall actually find a free PDF for a given article?
Unfortunately not all authors upload their fulltext PDFs online—although the number is growing every day. So sometimes there's no fulltext available online, and Unpaywall comes up empty. The exact percentage depends on the age and field of the article, but we generally find around 25% success. There's actually been a bunch of research on this recently, and we're planning a blog post breaking it down—stay tuned.
How's this different from Sci-Hub?
Like Unpaywall, Sci-Hub finds fulltext PDFs for paywalled articles. The main difference is where those PDFs come from: Unpaywall finds PDFs legally uploaded by the authors themselves, while Sci-Hub uses PDFs that are obtained by other means, including automated web scraping of publisher sites. Sci-Hub's method delivers more comprehensive results, but is not super legal. So while we're not against Sci-Hub, we think Unpaywall offers a more sustainable approach by working within copyright law and supporting the growing open access movement.
How do you find your fulltext articles?
We gather information from thousands of different open-access repositories with the help many awesome open data sources, especially the DOAJ, Crossref, DataCite, and BASE. We in turn make all this data open for reuse oaDOI API: a free, fast, and very scalable way to leverage our infrastructure to build your own projects.
I found a bug
Sorry about that! Unfortunately this is pretty common, especially where publishers don't follow standard practices for article display (as they often do not). For instance, we don't work for articles missing DOIs. The good news is that it's still early days for this project and it's under very active development. When you find an error, drop us an email and we'll get it fixed for you.

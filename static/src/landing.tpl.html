About

Ever find that perfect scholarly article, only to get stopped by a paywall? We can help.

Every time you hit a paywalled article, Unpaywall automatically looks for a copy in our index of over 20 million free, legal fulltext PDFs. If we find one, we give you a link to read. Open access has never been easier.

FAQ

Who is behind Unpaywall?
Unpaywall is from Impactstory, a nonprofit working to supercharge science by making it more open, reusable, and web-native.
Why did you make it?
We got tired of running into paywalled articles! The worst part was, we knew that millions of researchers are currently uploading their own fulltext PDFs to preprint servers and institutional repositories, worldwide, making them free for anyone to read. There was just no easy way to find them, especially not one that was integrated into our reading workflows. So we made one! Our long-term goal with Unpaywall is to nurture the transition to a fully open access scholarly publishing model, by closing the gap between readers and freely-available fulltext.
How do I use this?
After you install the extension we'll do the work in the background. When you view a paywalled article, we'll search for a free legal copy. If we find one, you'll see a bright green "free" tab on the right side of your browser window. Click on the tab, read the article. If you want to see an example of Unpaywall working, visit this article after you've installed the extension.
How often does Unpaywall actually find a free PDF for a given article?
Unfortunately not all authors upload their fulltext PDFs online—although the number is growing every day. So sometimes there's no fulltext available online, and Unpaywall comes up empty. The exact percentage depends on the age and field of the article, but we generally find around 40% success. There's actually been a bunch of research on this recently, and we're planning a blog post breaking it down—stay tuned.
Where does your data come from?
We gather information from thousands of different open-access repositories, with the help of a number of partners. In particular, we depend on data from the DOAJ, Crossref, DataCite, and (especially) BASE. All this data is available for free via the API of another Impactstory project, oaDOI. The oaDOI API is fully free, open, and scalable and lots of folks are building cool stuff on it already...check it out if you'd like to build something too!
How is this different from Sci-Hub?
Like Unpaywall, Sci-Hub finds fulltext PDFs for paywalled articles. The main difference is where those PDFs come from: Unpaywall finds PDFs legally uploaded by the authors themselves, while Sci-Hub uses PDFs that are obtained by other means including automated web scraping of publisher sites. Sci-Hub's method delivers more comprehensive results, but is not super legal. We're not against Sci-Hub. But we think Unpaywall offers a more sustainable approach, by working within copyright law and supporting the growing open access movement.
I found a bug
Sorry about that! Unfortunately this is not uncommon, mostly because scholarly publishing is still sort of a wild west of missing and ignored information standards, and this creates a number of problems for automatic systems like Unpaywall. The good news is that it's still early days for this project and it's under very active development. When you find an error, drop us an email and we'll get it fixed for you.
